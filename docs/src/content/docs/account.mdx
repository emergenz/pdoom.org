## Signing in

crowd-cast signs you in with **Google** (from **Sign in with Google** in the
[tray menu](/docs/crowd-cast/tray/#sign-in-with-google--sign-out), or during
first-run setup). Signing in opens your browser, you approve access, and you're
done.

Signing in links your uploaded recordings to your account so your contributions
can be **attributed to you** on the dashboard. If you don't sign in, recordings
can't be linked to you and won't show up on the dashboard.

**Sign out** from the tray menu removes your local sign-in. (To fully revoke
access, also remove crowd-cast from your Google account's security settings.)

## How uploads work

Finished [segments](/docs/crowd-cast/recording/#segments) upload in the background as
they complete, a couple at a time. While a segment is uploading the tray shows
*Uploading …*.

- Segments are stored **locally until they upload successfully**, then removed.
- If an upload fails (no connection, server hiccup), it's **retried
  automatically with exponential backoff** (from about 30 seconds up to 2 hours),
  so you don't have to do anything.
- You can **[Pause Uploads](/docs/crowd-cast/tray/#pause-uploads--resume-uploads)**
  from the tray to hold completed segments on disk (handy on a metered
  connection), and **Resume** later. Recording keeps going either way.

<Aside type="tip">
  If uploads seem stuck, check your connection and make sure uploads aren't
  paused. More in [troubleshooting](/docs/crowd-cast/troubleshooting/#uploads-are-failing).
</Aside>

## The dashboard

The **[dashboard](https://pdoom.org/crowd_cast_dashboard.html)** shows your
contributions and the overall collection progress. It updates roughly **once an
hour**, so freshly recorded data won't appear instantly.

If your recordings still aren't showing up after an hour, try signing out and
back in from the tray menu. See
[troubleshooting](/docs/crowd-cast/troubleshooting/#i-signed-in-but-cant-see-my-recordings).
