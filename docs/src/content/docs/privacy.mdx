crowd-cast is built so that you decide what gets recorded. This page is the
single place that spells out what's captured, what isn't, and how to keep
sensitive things out.

## What is captured

- **Screen video** of the applications you selected, while they're in the
  foreground.
- **Keyboard and mouse input** (keys, mouse movement, clicks, scroll), but only
  while a selected app is in the foreground.
- Basic metadata such as which app is focused and your display resolution.

## What is **not** captured

- **Anything from apps you didn't select.** When a non-selected app is in front,
  the video is a **black screen** and input logging stops.
- **Audio**: it's off by default.
- **Anything while you're idle.** crowd-cast pauses after 2 minutes without
  activity and records nothing until you return. See
  [idle detection](/docs/crowd-cast/recording/#idle-detection).

## How to keep things private

The model is an **allowlist**: only the apps you explicitly choose are ever
recorded. A few ways to use that:

- **Use a separate app for sensitive work.** The most reliable approach is to
  keep private activity in an app that simply isn't on the list.
- **Add only what you're comfortable sharing.** The more apps you record, the
  more useful the data, but it's entirely your call which ones.
- **Use the panic button.** If something sensitive slips into a recording, hit
  **[Delete last 10 minutes](/docs/crowd-cast/tray/#delete-last-10-minutes)** in the
  tray to discard recent, not-yet-uploaded footage.

<Aside type="tip" title="How we use it ourselves">
  We record one browser (e.g. Firefox) for research, a text editor (e.g.
  Cursor), a terminal (e.g. Ghostty) and a PDF viewer (e.g. Preview). We use a
  *separate* browser (e.g. Chrome) for sensitive things like email, messaging
  and contracts. That browser simply isn't on the list, so it's never
  recorded. When we switch to it, crowd-cast captures a black screen and stops
  logging input. We've found this covers most of the working day while keeping
  anything private completely out of the recording.
</Aside>

## Where your data goes

Finished segments upload to p(doom)'s research backend over secure, signed
URLs, and (when you're [signed in](/docs/crowd-cast/account/)) are attributed to your
account. Local copies are removed once they've uploaded. To understand how the
dataset is used, read the research write-up:
**[crowd-cast: Crowd-Sourcing Months-Long Trajectories of Human Computer Work](https://pdoom.org/crowd_cast.html)**.
