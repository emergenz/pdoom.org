crowd-cast records the apps you selected during setup and nothing else. This
page explains exactly how that works.

## What gets captured: only your selected apps

Two separate mechanisms decide what ends up in a recording. They give different
guarantees, so it helps to keep them apart.

### Video is enforced by the compositor

crowd-cast does not record your whole screen and then crop it down. It asks the
operating system to capture only the **specific applications you selected**: on
macOS through ScreenCaptureKit's application-capture mode, on Linux/X11 through
XComposite, and on Linux/Wayland through the desktop portal's ScreenCast window
source. The pixels of any app you did not select never reach crowd-cast at all,
so an unselected app cannot appear in the video, even by accident. That guarantee
is enforced by the compositor or window manager **underneath crowd-cast**, not by
crowd-cast deciding what to keep after the fact.

When the foreground app is not one you selected, the video is a **blank (black)
screen**. Switch back to a selected app and it resumes.

<Aside type="note" title="Linux">
  On Linux/X11 this works through XComposite, and on Wayland through the desktop
  portal, which mediates access and prompts per app, so the same guarantee holds.
  The exception is wlroots-based compositors (sway, Hyprland), whose portal only
  offers full-screen capture; there, only the whole screen can be recorded, so it
  is your decision whether to record at all on such a setup. See the
  [Linux compatibility matrix](/docs/crowd-cast/linux/#compatibility-matrix).
</Aside>

### Which monitor

On **macOS**, crowd-cast captures the **main display** (the monitor set as
primary in your display settings). On a multi-monitor setup only that monitor is
captured, so a recorded app's window on a secondary monitor is not.

On **Windows**, a selected app is captured on **whichever monitor it is on**. The
segment is a single canvas sized to fit all of your monitors at native
resolution, with black bars wherever they don't tile evenly, and the app is
placed at its real position on that canvas. As everywhere, only your selected
apps appear and everything else is black. crowd-cast also records the moments
when focus moves from one monitor to another, so the native, single-monitor view
can be reconstructed losslessly in post-processing.

On **Linux**, which monitor is used depends on your session; see the
[Linux guide](/docs/crowd-cast/linux/#which-monitor).

### Follow-focus is only for input

Keyboard and mouse events are delivered system-wide, not per window, so something
has to decide **when** to record them. That is the one job follow-focus does:
crowd-cast tracks which app is in front (about ten times a second) and logs input
**only while one of your selected apps is focused**. The moment you switch to
anything else, input logging stops.

So follow-focus governs **input**, not the safety of the video. Even if focus
detection ever misfired, the worst case would be mis-timed input, never another
app's screen, because the video is already constrained by the compositor as
described above. This is the core of crowd-cast's
[privacy model](/docs/crowd-cast/privacy/).

<Aside type="note" title="One app at a time (macOS and X11)">
  The frontmost app also decides which of your selected apps is the one actively
  recorded at any moment (crowd-cast records one at a time to avoid redundant
  capture). This only ever switches between apps you chose; it can never bring in
  an app you didn't.
</Aside>

## Segments

Recordings are split into **segments** (5 minutes each by default). Each finished
segment (a video file plus its input-event log) is uploaded on its own. This
is also why **Settings** changes take effect on the *next* segment rather than
mid-recording.

## Idle detection

If there's **no keyboard or mouse activity for 2 minutes**, crowd-cast
automatically **pauses** both video and input. The status shows *Idle
(paused)*. As soon as you move the mouse or press a key, it resumes. **No data
is recorded while you're idle**, so stepping away from your desk never gets
captured.

## What gets captured

- **Video** of the selected app's window, encoded efficiently (HEVC/H.265 with
  hardware acceleration where available, downscaled to 1080p, 30 fps). Audio is
  **not** recorded.
- **Input events**: key presses/releases and mouse movement, clicks and scroll,
  each timestamped, but only while a selected app is in the foreground.

For the full picture of what is and isn't collected, and how to keep things
private, see [Privacy & data](/docs/crowd-cast/privacy/).

## Reliability

The capture engine is watched by a watchdog: if the underlying capture call
stalls, crowd-cast restarts itself cleanly to recover rather than sitting in a
broken state. You may occasionally see the tray icon briefly disappear and
reappear when this happens.
