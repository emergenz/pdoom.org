The first time you launch crowd-cast, a **setup wizard** opens automatically. It
walks through three things: granting permissions, choosing which apps to record,
and whether to start at login. When it finishes, recording starts automatically;
signing in (to attribute your contributions) is a separate step you do from the
tray menu afterwards.

The wizard also reopens automatically later if a required permission is missing.
You can re-run it any time. See [re-running the wizard](/docs/crowd-cast/troubleshooting/#re-run-the-setup-wizard).

## 1. Permissions

<Tabs syncKey="os">
<TabItem label="macOS">

crowd-cast needs two macOS permissions. The wizard requests both and won't be
able to record properly until they're granted:

- **Screen Recording**: required to capture windows. Without it, recording
  starts but there are no capture sources.
- **Accessibility**: required to capture keyboard and mouse input. Without it,
  video records but no input is logged.

If you skip one, grant it later in **System Settings → Privacy & Security**
under the matching section, then restart crowd-cast.

</TabItem>
<TabItem label="Windows">

No special permissions are required on Windows. The wizard goes straight to
choosing which apps to record.

</TabItem>
<TabItem label="Linux">

crowd-cast supports two Linux sessions: **GNOME on Wayland** (application
capture) and **sway** (full-screen capture). On anything else the wizard won't
let you finish, so log into one of those first. Input is captured via `evdev`,
which requires your user to be in the `input` group; the installer handles this
for you (it's the one step that needs `sudo`). On **GNOME**, launch crowd-cast
once before logging out and follow the wizard's instruction to install the
bundled focus extension. Then log out and back in once: that single relogin
activates both the `input` group membership and the GNOME extension.

</TabItem>
</Tabs>

## 2. Choose which apps to record

The wizard asks whether to **capture all applications** or to pick a specific
set. If you pick a set, **only those apps are captured**: when a non-selected app
is in the foreground, crowd-cast records a black screen and stops logging input.
It is a one-time step: a selected app is remembered and keeps being captured on
later launches, even after you close and reopen it.

What the app list shows depends on your platform:

<Tabs syncKey="os">
<TabItem label="macOS">

The list shows the applications **currently running**. To record one that isn't
open, launch it first, then select it.

</TabItem>
<TabItem label="Windows">

The list shows the applications **currently running**. To record one that isn't
open, launch it first, then select it.

</TabItem>
<TabItem label="Linux">

The list shows the applications **currently running** (on Wayland, applications
that have an open window). To record one that isn't open, launch it first, then
select it.

</TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Aside type="tip">
  You can change this selection at any time later from **Settings** in the tray
  menu. Saving applies it immediately: recording restarts with the new selection.
  For the privacy rationale (and our own setup), see [Privacy & data](/docs/crowd-cast/privacy/).
</Aside>

## 3. Start at login

The wizard offers to **start crowd-cast automatically on login**. We recommend
keeping this on so the agent runs whenever you log in without you having to
remember to launch it.

## Done

When you finish the wizard, the app restarts cleanly and its icon appears in
your **menu bar** (macOS) or **system tray** (Windows/Linux). Recording starts
automatically.

## Sign in with Google

The wizard does **not** ask you to sign in. Once setup is finished and crowd-cast
is running, open the tray menu and choose **Sign in with Google**. This links
your recordings to your account on the
[dashboard](https://pdoom.org/crowd_cast_dashboard.html) so your contributions
can be attributed to you. Recording works whether or not you sign in, but without
it your recordings can't be linked to you and won't appear on the dashboard.

See [Account, uploads & dashboard](/docs/crowd-cast/account/) for how sign-in
works and what it does. Everything from here on is controlled from
[the tray menu](/docs/crowd-cast/tray/).
