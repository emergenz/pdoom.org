The first time you launch crowd-cast, a **setup wizard** opens automatically. It
walks through three things: granting permissions, choosing which apps to record,
and whether to start at login. After that, you sign in and you're recording.

The wizard also reopens automatically later if a required permission is missing.
You can re-run it any time. See [re-running the wizard](/docs/crowd-cast/troubleshooting/#re-run-the-setup-wizard).

## 1. Permissions

<Tabs syncKey="os">
<TabItem label="macOS">

crowd-cast needs two macOS permissions. The wizard requests both and won't be
able to record properly until they're granted:

- **Screen Recording**: required to capture windows. Without it, recording
  starts but there are no capture sources.
- **Accessibility**: required to capture keyboard and mouse input. Without it,
  video records but no input is logged.

If you skip one, grant it later in **System Settings → Privacy & Security**
under the matching section, then restart crowd-cast.

</TabItem>
<TabItem label="Windows">

No special permissions are required on Windows. The wizard goes straight to
choosing which apps to record.

</TabItem>
<TabItem label="Linux">

On Wayland, crowd-cast captures input via `evdev`, which requires your user to
be in the `input` group. The Linux installer handles this for you (it's the one
step that needs `sudo`). See the [Linux guide](/docs/crowd-cast/linux/#input-capture)
for details. Per-app capture and follow-focus availability depend on your
display server and compositor. See the [compatibility matrix](/docs/crowd-cast/linux/#compatibility-matrix).

</TabItem>
</Tabs>

## 2. Choose which apps to record

<Steps>

1. The wizard asks whether to **capture all applications**, or to pick specific
   ones.

2. If you pick specific apps, it lists the applications currently running and
   you select the ones you want recorded (for example your browser, terminal,
   IDE and PDF viewer).

3. Only the apps you select are captured. **Everything else is ignored**: when
   a non-selected app is in the foreground, crowd-cast records a black screen
   and stops logging input.

</Steps>

<Aside type="tip">
  You can change this selection at any time later from **Settings** in the tray
  menu. Changes take effect on the next recording segment. For the privacy
  rationale (and our own setup), see [Privacy & data](/docs/crowd-cast/privacy/).
</Aside>

## 3. Start at login

The wizard offers to **start crowd-cast automatically on login**. We recommend
keeping this on so the agent runs whenever you log in without you having to
remember to launch it.

## 4. Sign in with Google

When prompted, **sign in with Google**. This links your recordings to your
account on the [dashboard](https://pdoom.org/crowd_cast_dashboard.html) so your
contributions can be attributed to you. Without signing in, recordings can't be
linked to you and won't appear on the dashboard.

See [Account, uploads & dashboard](/docs/crowd-cast/account/) for how sign-in works
and what it does.

## Done

When you finish the wizard, the app restarts cleanly and its icon appears in
your **menu bar** (macOS) or **system tray** (Windows/Linux). Recording starts
automatically. Everything from here on is controlled from
[the tray menu](/docs/crowd-cast/tray/).
