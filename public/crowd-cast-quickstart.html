Quickstart

Get crowd-cast running in under five minutes. Install, grant permissions, and you're recording.

Download for macOS Download for Windows

Install

  1. Open CrowdCast.dmg and drag the app to your Applications folder.
  2. Launch CrowdCast from Applications. The setup wizard opens automatically on first run.
  3. The wizard asks for two macOS permissions. Grant both:
    1. Screen Recording (required to capture windows)
    2. Accessibility (required for keyboard and mouse input)
  4. Configure your preferences in the wizard:
    1. Select the apps you want to record (e.g. your browser, terminal, IDE). Only selected apps are captured. Everything else is ignored.
    2. Enable "Start at login" so crowd-cast runs automatically whenever you log in. We recommend keeping this on.
  5. Sign in with Google when prompted. This links your recordings to your account on the dashboard so we can track your contributions.
  6. Click Done. The app restarts and appears as a small icon in your menu bar. Recording starts automatically.

Install

  1. Run crowd-cast-setup.exe. The installer places the app under your user profile (no admin or UAC prompt required).
  2. SmartScreen warning: Because the app is not yet widely distributed, Windows may show "Windows protected your PC". Click More info, then Run anyway.
  3. Configure your preferences in the setup wizard:
    1. Select the apps you want to record. Only selected apps are captured. Everything else is ignored.
    2. Enable "Start at login" so crowd-cast runs automatically whenever you log in. We recommend keeping this on.
  4. Sign in with Google when prompted. This links your recordings to your account on the dashboard so we can track your contributions.
  5. On first launch, crowd-cast downloads OBS components automatically (one-time, needs internet). The app relaunches itself when done. This takes about 30 to 60 seconds.
    Don't close the app during the initial OBS download. If interrupted, delete the install folder and re-run the installer.
  6. After the relaunch, crowd-cast appears in your system tray (bottom-right, near the clock). Recording starts automatically.
How we use it: We record one browser (e.g. Firefox) for research, a text editor (e.g. Cursor), a terminal (e.g. Ghostty), and a PDF viewer (e.g. Preview). We use a separate browser (e.g. Chrome) for sensitive things like email, messaging, and contracts. That browser is simply not in the list, so it never gets recorded. When you switch to an app that isn't selected, crowd-cast captures a black screen and stops logging input. The more apps you record, the more useful the data, so add anything you're comfortable sharing. We found this setup covers the majority of the working day while keeping anything private completely out of the recording.

Using crowd-cast

Everything is controlled from the tray icon in your menu bar (macOS) or system tray (Windows).

Start / Stop Recording
Click the tray icon and choose Start or Stop. A green dot means recording is active.
Delete last 10 minutes
The panic button. Deletes recent recordings that haven't been uploaded yet.
Pause / Resume Uploads
Temporarily holds completed segments on disk instead of uploading them.
Settings
Change which apps are recorded. Takes effect on the next segment.
Sign in with Google
Please sign in so your recordings appear on the dashboard. Without signing in, your contributions can't be attributed to you.
Idle detection: crowd-cast automatically pauses when you step away (no keyboard or mouse activity for 2 minutes) and resumes when you return. No data is recorded while you're idle.

Troubleshooting

Recording shows "no capture sources"

macOS: Screen Recording permission was denied or revoked. Open System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording, find CrowdCast, and toggle it on. You may need to quit and relaunch the app.

Windows: No special permissions are needed. If you see this, the OBS download may have failed. Check your internet connection, delete the install folder at %LOCALAPPDATA%\Programs\crowd-cast, and re-run the installer.

The tray icon doesn't appear

macOS: Check if the app is running in Activity Monitor. If it is, the icon may be hidden. Look for the overflow area (arrow) on the right side of the menu bar.

Windows: Click the ^ arrow in the system tray to see hidden icons. You can drag crowd-cast to the always-visible area.

Uploads are failing

Segments are retried automatically with exponential backoff (30 seconds up to 2 hours). Check your internet connection. If uploads are paused, resume them from the tray menu. Segments are stored locally until they upload successfully.

I want to re-run the setup wizard

macOS: Quit crowd-cast, then run from Terminal:
/Applications/CrowdCast.app/Contents/MacOS/crowd-cast-agent --setup

Windows: Quit crowd-cast from the tray, then run from Command Prompt or PowerShell:
"%LOCALAPPDATA%\Programs\crowd-cast\crowd-cast-agent.exe" --setup

I signed in but can't see my recordings on the dashboard

The dashboard updates roughly once per hour, so new recordings won't appear immediately. If your data still doesn't show up after an hour, try signing out and back in from the tray menu. If that doesn't help either, reach out to us.

Where are the logs?

macOS: ~/Library/Logs/crowd-cast/

Windows: %LOCALAPPDATA%\crowd-cast\logs\

Share these with us on Discord if you need help debugging.

How do I uninstall?

macOS: Quit the app, then drag CrowdCast from Applications to the Trash. To also remove configuration and logs: delete ~/Library/Application Support/dev.crowd-cast.agent/ and ~/Library/Logs/crowd-cast/.

Windows: Open Settings → Apps → Installed apps, find crowd-cast, and click Uninstall. The uninstaller stops the agent, removes the autostart entry, and deletes the install directory.
