We are p(doom), an AGI research lab in Munich. Our goal is models that can perform complex work over weeks and months, and that takes training data nobody has: recordings of real, long-horizon work. crowd-cast is our open-source desktop recorder built for exactly that. Over 3,000 hours recorded so far by the team and early participants, watch it grow on the live dashboard.

We pay accepted participants $300 per month to record their normal screen work: research, engineering, coding, CAD and hardware workflows, thesis or PhD projects, serious side projects. We especially want the work current AI can't do yet. It has to be work you have the right to share, your own projects and research, not your employer's confidential material.

You keep working exactly as before. No tasks, no deadlines, no meetings: you are selling recordings of work you were doing anyway, not your labor.

How it works
  • 01Apply through the signup form.
  • 02If accepted, install crowd-cast on macOS, Windows, or Linux.
  • 03On first launch, you choose which applications get recorded. Everything outside that list is automatically excluded.
  • 04We capture five-minute segments and upload to a private S3 bucket. The local copy is deleted on each segment boundary.
Privacy & trust

Only the applications you select are captured; everything else, including pop-ups and notifications, is excluded. You can pause or stop recording at any time from the UI, and deletion requests are honored, including derived data.

Nothing here is take-our-word-for-it: the recorder is open source, and the exact agreements you'd accept are public before you apply: the Data Purchase Agreement and the Privacy & Recording Consent (GDPR). p(doom) is publicly funded by SPRIND, the German Federal Agency for Breakthrough Innovation.
